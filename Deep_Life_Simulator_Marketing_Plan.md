# Deep Life Simulator — Organic Marketing Plan

**Date:** March 9, 2026
**Budget:** $0 (organic only)
**Status:** Live on iOS App Store
**Current rating:** 2.3 stars (8 reviews)

---

## The Honest Starting Point

Before talking tactics, here's the truth: **your 2.3-star rating is the single biggest thing holding back growth right now.** Every marketing effort you make will funnel people to your App Store page, and most will bounce at 2.3 stars. So the plan below starts with fixing the foundation before driving traffic.

---

## Phase 1: Fix the Foundation (Weeks 1–3)

### 1.1 Squash the Showstopper Bugs

Multiple reviews mention bugs that prevent weekly progression and overly frequent diseases. These are retention killers. Before anything else, ship a stability patch that addresses the top complaints visible in your reviews. A game that works well generates good word-of-mouth for free.

**Action items:**

- Audit every 1- and 2-star review for specific bug reports
- Prioritize fixes that block core gameplay (week progression, disease balance)
- Ship a focused bug-fix update (don't bundle new features — let the patch notes say "we listened")

### 1.2 Respond to Every Review

Apple lets you respond to reviews. Do it. Thank positive reviewers. Acknowledge bug reports on negative reviews and let them know a fix is coming. This signals to potential downloaders that the developer is active and cares.

### 1.3 Set Up a Rating Prompt

If you don't already have one, add an in-app review prompt (using Apple's `SKStoreReviewController`) that triggers after a positive gameplay moment — like completing a major career milestone, buying a first car, or getting married. Happy players asked at the right moment leave good reviews.

**Target:** Get to 4.0+ stars and 30+ reviews before investing heavily in traffic.

---

## Phase 2: App Store Optimization (Weeks 2–4)

ASO is your #1 organic growth lever. It's free and compounds over time.

### 2.1 Title & Subtitle

Your current title is "Deep Life Simulator." That's solid — it contains "Life Simulator" which is a high-volume keyword. For the subtitle (30 characters max), target keywords that BitLife users search for.

**Suggested subtitle options:**

- "Career, Money & Life Choices"
- "Build Your Life From Nothing"
- "Realistic Life Strategy Game"

### 2.2 Keyword Field (100 characters)

Apple gives you 100 characters for keywords (comma-separated, no spaces after commas). Focus on terms your competitors rank for.

**Suggested keyword string:**
`life simulator,tycoon,career,money,business,investing,choices,strategy,idle,management,bitlife,sim`

### 2.3 Screenshots That Sell

Your screenshots should tell a story. The first three are the most important (they show before the user taps "see more"). Each screenshot should have a bold headline at the top explaining the benefit, not just showing gameplay.

**Recommended screenshot sequence:**

1. "Start With Nothing. Build Everything." — Show the starting scenario selection
2. "Climb the Career Ladder" — Show career progression or salary milestones
3. "Buy Cars, Houses & More" — Show the vehicle/property system
4. "Invest, Save, Go Bankrupt" — Show the financial mechanics
5. "Your Choices Have Real Consequences" — Show a dramatic life event (wedding, divorce, etc.)

### 2.4 App Description

Lead with your differentiator. Most life sim games are shallow text-based games. Your game has real economic mechanics. Make that clear in the first three lines (the part visible before "more").

**Suggested opening:**
> Unlike other life simulators, Deep Life Simulator features a real economic engine — loans, debt, bankruptcy, investing, and compound growth. Every financial decision matters. Start with nothing and see if you can build generational wealth, or watch it all collapse from one bad choice.

### 2.5 App Preview Video

Record a 15–30 second preview video showing the gameplay loop: starting a new life → getting a job → buying your first car → a dramatic life event. Apple auto-plays these on the listing page and they significantly boost conversion rates. You can screen-record on your iPhone and edit in iMovie for free.

## Phase 3: Content & Community (Weeks 3–8)

### 3.1 TikTok (Your Highest-ROI Channel)

The hashtag #LifeSimulator has 32+ million views on TikTok. This is where your audience already lives.

**Content ideas (all free to produce):**

- **"I tried to get rich in a life simulator"** — Screen record a playthrough where you chase wealth. Show the highs and the crash. People love watching financial drama.
- **"What happens if you make EVERY bad decision"** — Deliberately go bankrupt, get divorced, lose everything. The trainwreck is the content.
- **"This game actually simulates real economics"** — Show the loan/interest/investing mechanics. Position it as "BitLife but realistic."
- **"Day 1 vs Day 100 in Deep Life Simulator"** — Before/after comparison of a character's progression.
- **Behind-the-scenes dev content** — People love seeing indie developers building their games. Show your coding setup, talk about design decisions, share your journey.

**Posting cadence:** 3–5 short videos per week. Batch-record on weekends, post throughout the week. Use trending sounds.

**Key principle:** Don't make ads. Make content that people would watch even if they didn't know it was about your game. The game sells itself when the content is entertaining.

### 3.2 Reddit

Reddit communities can drive significant spikes in downloads when done right. The key is being a genuine community member, not a marketer.

**Subreddits to participate in:**

- **r/iosgaming** — Post a "developer here, just shipped a major update" thread when you have a good update. Be honest about where the game is and where it's going.
- **r/incremental_games** — If your game has prestige/idle mechanics, this community is perfect. They love discovering new games.
- **r/IndieGaming** — Share development progress, screenshots, and milestones.
- **r/gamedev** — Share your marketing journey and learnings. Developers love rooting for other developers, and they'll check out your game.
- **r/AndroidGaming** — Once you launch on Android, announce it here.

**Rules:** Never just drop a link. Write a genuine post about what makes your game different, what you learned building it, or ask for feedback. Engage in comments.

### 3.3 YouTube

You don't need to be a YouTuber yourself, but you should make it easy for YouTubers to cover your game.

**Actions:**

- Create a simple press kit page (can be a free Notion page) with: game description, screenshots, logo, key features, and a trailer/gameplay video
- Search YouTube for "life simulator game" videos and find small creators (1K–50K subscribers) who review mobile games
- Reach out to 10–20 of them with a short, friendly message: "Hey, I'm an indie dev who built a life simulator with real economics. Would you be interested in covering it?" Don't be pushy. Small creators are often excited to find new games to review.

### 3.4 Discord Community

Create a free Discord server for your game. This serves three purposes: it gives your most engaged players a place to connect, it gives you a direct feedback channel, and it creates a community that markets itself through word-of-mouth.

**Suggested channels:** #announcements, #general, #bug-reports, #feature-requests, #screenshots (let players share their in-game achievements)

## Phase 4: Retention & Growth Loops (Ongoing)

### 4.1 Build a Share Mechanic

Add a "Share Your Life" button that lets players share a screenshot or summary card of their character's life story to social media. Make it visually interesting — show the character's net worth, career, age, and key life events. This turns every player into a potential marketer.

### 4.2 Update Cadence

Ship visible updates every 2–3 weeks. Each update gives you a reason to post on social media, respond to community feedback, and show the game is actively developed. A live, evolving game retains players and attracts new ones.

**Update communication template:**
- What's new (the exciting stuff first)
- What's fixed (acknowledge known issues)
- What's next (tease upcoming features to keep people engaged)

### 4.3 Seasonal Events

Tie in-game events to real-world moments — holiday bonuses, tax season challenges, back-to-school scenarios. These create urgency and give you timely content to post about.

### 4.4 Cross-Promotion

Find other indie life sim or tycoon developers and cross-promote. You can mention each other's games in your communities, Discord servers, or social media. This costs nothing and exposes your game to a pre-qualified audience.

## Phase 5: Android Launch (When Ready)

Your game appears to be iOS-only right now. Android represents roughly 70% of the global mobile market. Launching on Google Play is essentially doubling your addressable audience for free (since you're already built on React Native/Expo).

**When you launch on Android:**

- Announce it everywhere (all your social channels, Reddit, Discord)
- Google Play has different ASO rules — the description is searchable, so stuff it with relevant keywords naturally
- Reach out to Android-focused gaming creators
